INFO How Aux Affects Sizing
Efficiency Chain Impact

Auxiliary loads (HVAC, BMS, fire suppression, lighting) consume power from the battery system, reducing the energy available at the Point of Interconnection (POI).

Sizing Flow:
Required Energy @ POI
  ÷ Total Bat→POI Efficiency
  ÷ Battery Loss Factor
  = Required DC Energy

Aux Deduction (when source = Battery):
Total Aux Power = Links × Peak kW per LINK
Dischargeable Energy = DC Energy − Aux Power × Duration
  ÷ DC-to-Aux Efficiency

Aux Power Source = Battery: Aux load is subtracted from the battery's dischargeable energy, increasing the required number of LINKs.

Aux Power Source = Grid: Aux load is supplied externally. No impact on battery sizing. The DC-to-Aux Efficiency path is still used for the efficiency chain calculation.
